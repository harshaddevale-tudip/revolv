    Now comes the fun part…making a deal! Once we’ve collected all this information, RE-volv will be able to find local installers, and put a proposal and lease agreement together. With your help, we’ll sign a lease agreement in no time!
    A. Solar installers, Site Visits, and Lease Agreements
    Once we have information about the building and how much energy they use, RE-volv will reach out to a series of solar installers in the area and ask for bids on a project. Once we’ve selected a reputable solar installer, they’ll need to do a site visit. As the Solar Champion your job will be to coordinate with the nonprofit and the installer to find an appropriate time to do a site inspection. The solar installer will come to the nonprofit, take measurements and ask any final questions they need to complete their proposal. Then they’ll provide RE-volv with a formal proposal. Once we have that, RE-volv will create a solar proposal for the nonprofit and a 20 year lease agreement.
    B. Call with champions and RE-volv to go over the lease
    RE-volv will email the solar proposal and solar lease agreement to the point of contact at the nonprofit. Once they’ve had a chance to read through the documents we’ll set up a phone call with you and your team, the nonprofit, and RE-volv staff. This will give the nonprofit the opportunity to ask any clarifying questions regarding the lease and RE-volv the opportunity to explain all the details.
    C. If they agree they sign a lease
    Hopefully, after our meeting and any internal discussions they need to have, the nonprofit will decide to sign the lease! Then we’ll need the nonprofit to print out two copies of the lease agreement, sign them, and mail them to RE-volv. Then we’ll sign them and send one of them back to them. That way we both have signed copies of the lease agreement. Then it’s time to start crowdfunding!
