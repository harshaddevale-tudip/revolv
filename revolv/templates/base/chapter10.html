    The hard work is all done! Now it’s time for the fun part! Time to celebrate!
    A. Planning a Ribbon Cutting Event
    Hopefully you’ve already been planning this event, but now it’s time to formalize the details. Sit down with the nonprofit staff to find a good date and time to host the event. Figure out what food and drinks you’re going to get, who you’re going to invite to speak, and how you’re going to get the word out to the community.
    B. Inviting politicians
    Local elected officials love events like this. Whether it’s someone from the mayor’s office, your state legislature, or even your federal representatives, politicians love opportunities to get in front of their constituents and talk about things they care about. Hopefully you have some local officials that care about clean energy and the environment, or perhaps officials that champion the cause of your nonprofit. In any event, inviting the official to speak is fairly easy. Typically there is a link on the officials website specifically to invite them to events like this. Otherwise, just calling their staff and telling them the date and time of the event is often sufficient. They’ll let you know if they can make it. The good thing about getting some officials or well respected members of the community to speak at the event is that you can put that on the invitation and get more people to come to your event.
    C. Inviting the press
    Once you have the date, time, and location confirmed, you can start reaching out to the press with a Media Advisory. This is a document that is similar to a Press Release but it is specifically geared towards events. You’ll want to make some phone calls as well in the days leading up to the event and even the day of to let the news organizations know. They might be looking for something to cover at that time. If you have lined up politicians to speak at the event, the press is much more likely to come and write a story about the project.
    D. Inviting the community
    Similar to your previous events you’re going to want to publicize the event through email invitations, social media, flyers and posters, and word of mouth. Since celebrating the solar installation is a big milestone for the community, hopefully there will be lots of people excited to come and celebrate with you!
    E. Invite the Solar Installer
    At events like these you’re going to have people who are excited to learn more about solar energy. It’s a great opportunity to get more people to go solar at home! Invite the solar installer to join the event and have a table so that they can talk to attendees about their service and sign them up for free quotes.
    Pro Tip:
    At the end of the day, this entire process is about getting people excited about solar energy and also to realize that solar is accessible, and is already benefiting the community. We don’t have to wait for clean energy to come to our community down the road. Clean energy is already happening! In our community!
    F. Keep us in the loop!
    Lastly, remember that RE-volv staff and past Solar Champions are here to help. Many people have gone through this exact process before and we know exactly what you’re going through. If you come across a snag, let us know and we’ll see if we can offer any guidance. If you need a hand, we’re happy to help. A lot of the challenges you’ll be coming across often have easy solutions that we may have learned already, so please ask. We’re here to help you succeed. Best of luck!
