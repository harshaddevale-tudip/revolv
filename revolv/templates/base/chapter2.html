    CHAPTER 2:
    Your Mission,
    if you Choose to Accept
    Here’s what it takes to bring solar to your community.

    Before we get into the nitty gritty, let’s be honest. Leading a solar project in your community is no easy task. But solving climate change isn’t going to be easy. It’s going to take lots of people doing hard work for a long time to change our energy system. But it starts here. And we hope you’re willing to join us in the effort.
    A. Put a team together
    Leading a solar project can be a lot of work. You’re going to need a trustworthy team to help. Preferably at least 3-5 people will be a part of your core team of people that are going to lead the project with you. One person will be the Project Lead, and the others will all typically take ownership of a certain area. For example, someone can be in charge of social media, someone else, reaching out to the press, a third person focused on events. Your team can have as many people as you’d like, but you’ll want at least 3-5 core people on your team.
    B. Find a nonprofit to solarize
    So where exactly is this solar project going to go? Well, we’re hoping that you can help us figure that out! We’d like you to find a nonprofit organization (or a cooperative) that serves your community. It could be a homeless shelter, a nonprofit school, a house of worship, a community center, a food kitchen, an arts center, a food co-op, you name it! (We’ll get into more specific details in chapter 3.) Once you’ve found a suitable nonprofit, RE-volv will work closely with you to prepare and present a lease agreement to the nonprofit.
    C. Run a crowdfunding campaign
    Once a lease is signed, it’s time to raise the money! You and your team will make a video, host events, and raise the money needed to install the solar project. While this may seem daunting, it’s actually a lot of fun! More to come on this process in the later chapters.
    D. Document the installation
    When the installation happens, we’re going to ask that you and your team help capture the moment. Taking video footage, photos, and writing blogs about the install will all be greatly appreciated!
    E. Celebrate!
    Once the installation is complete it’s time to celebrate! You and your team and the nonprofit will set up a ribbon cutting event and invite the community to join. Hopefully some local community leaders can come say a few words and the press will come to report on the story. That way we reach even more people with the good news about solar!
